The picks and shovels, re-rated.

Hubbell makes the grid hardware — distribution insulators, arresters, cutouts, Aclara meters, Burndy connectors, PCX power skids — that utilities and data-center operators consume by the truckload. Q1 2026 raised the FY26 sales guide +150 bps on data-center acceleration (>25%) and grid-infrastructure strength (+12%) — but the EPS range only narrowed at the floor and margin expansion was downgraded. The stock sits at 97% of all-time highs; the bear case is the asymmetry.

01 / The setup

A specialty manufacturer with two engines and one drag.

Thesis

Hubbell sells the unglamorous hardware that keeps the U.S. grid running and the inside of buildings wired. Two reportable segments: Utility Solutions (~63% of FY25 revenue) — splitting into Grid Infrastructure (the physical T&D hardware, ~75% of HUS) and Grid Automation (Aclara metering + protection, ~25%) — and Electrical Solutions (~37%) — Burndy connectors, PCX modular power skids for hyperscale data centers, industrial enclosures.

Q1 2026 was a clean beat-and-raise. Total sales $1.517B (+11%), adjusted EPS $3.93 (+16%), adjusted OP margin 19.8% (+110 bps), book-to-bill ~1.2×. Management raised the FY26 sales guide to +8% to +11% (from +7–9%), organic growth to +6% to +9%, data-center growth to >25% (from mid/high-teens), and lifted the EPS floor to $19.30–$19.85.

The single negative is Aclara/Grid Automation, still down −7% YoY in Q1 2026 after Q3 2025's −18% trough. Management's 2025 "return to growth in Q4" goalpost slipped four times; the framing has now retreated to "stabilization at a lower base, modest growth from the bottom." Margin guide also softened: FY26 margin expansion is now described as "slightly more modest" than the original ~+50 bps thanks to inflation and growth investments.

"Raising our full-year sales growth outlook to 8%–11% … organic sales growth outlook to 6%–9% … data center markets to more than 25% … $19.30–$19.85 per share." Management, Q1 2026 Earnings Call (April 30, 2026)
02 / Revenue

Two segments, both growing.

Composition

Utility Solutions printed $3.67B FY25 revenue on +6% YoY growth, with Q4'25 inflecting to +10% as grid-infrastructure orders broke higher. Q1 2026 carried the trend: Utility +11% reported (organic +7%), Grid Infrastructure organic +12%, Grid Automation still −7%. Electrical Solutions printed $2.17B FY25 on +13% YoY (mostly from mid-2024's industrial-products acquisitions plus accelerating data-center demand). Q1 2026 Electrical +12%, organic +11%, data center +40%.

The model carries FY26 revenue at $6.40B (+9.5%), the midpoint of the raised guide range, and FY27 at $6.82B (+6.5%). Both engines compound: Utility on T&D + the new 765kV transmission TAM ($1.5B over 10 years) introduced this quarter; Electrical on data center plus light-industrial pull-through.

03 / Data center

The single largest upside in the model.

Acceleration

Hubbell's data-center exposure runs through Electrical Solutions — primarily the PCX modular power skid / e-house business plus Burndy connectors and industrial enclosures consumed inside the data hall. PCX builds factory-finished switchgear modules shipped to hyperscaler campuses on 9–18-month lead times; the customer book is concentrated, the lead-time lock-in is real.

The growth ramp is the most visible structural driver in the company. Q4 2025 printed +60%, Q1 2026 +40%, and management raised the FY26 guide from mid/high-teens to >25% — the largest single-driver guide raise in the window. Long-cycle modular skid demand is largely booked; the binding constraint is short-cycle capacity adds.

04 / Grid infrastructure

The quiet compounder.

Driver

Inside Utility Solutions, Grid Infrastructure is the ~75% piece that compounds with U.S. utility T&D capex — distribution insulators, arresters, cutouts, transformer accessories, and the substation hardware that goes with them. Grid Infrastructure grew +12% organic in both Q4 2025 and Q1 2026 on broad-based transmission + substation + distribution strength.

Two new structural pieces showed up this quarter. First, a 765kV transmission TAM of $1.5B over the next 10 years — incremental to existing 345kV strength, with several early project wins already closed. Second, sustained order strength at book-to-bill ~1.2× across Utility — strong enough to drive the FY26 organic guide raise from 5–7% to high single digit.

The drag is Grid Automation / Aclara — the AMI metering business Hubbell acquired from ESCO in 2018. After 2024–25 destocking, management's recovery framing slipped four times: Q2'25 "return to growth in Q4" → Q3'25 "fade expected Q4" → Q4'25 "stabilized at lower base" → Q1'26 "−7%, slight Q2 growth expected." The latest line is "stabilization, muni/co-op focus, modest growth from a lower base" — recovery language has been quietly dropped.

05 / Earnings

Compounding at ~9% through the cycle.

Trajectory

Adjusted EPS has compounded from $15.32 in FY23 to $18.33 in FY25 (+9.4% CAGR). Management's raised FY26 guide of $19.30–$19.85 implies +5.3% to +8.3% YoY growth — the lower than-headline compounding rate reflects the modest margin-expansion downgrade plus higher net interest from the DMC acquisition and tariff working capital. The model carries FY26e at the high end ($19.85) and FY27e at $21.835, supported by ~10% operating-profit growth and a 53.1M share count.

A point worth flagging: the 2026 EPS print is essentially all operational — buybacks are EPS-neutral in 2026 due to higher interest expense, and management framed buyback accretion as "a 2027 story." If grid-automation finally inflects positive in Q2 and margin expansion holds at ≥75 bps, the high end of guide is comfortably reachable; if the margin walk degrades further, the model lands in the lower half.

06 / Margins

A 21% OI-margin business with a softer 2026 walk.

Mechanics

HUBB has structurally higher operating margins than the EPC names — utility specification lock-in plus pricing power on the connector / arrester / insulator portfolio. FY25 GAAP operating margin reached 20.7% (vs 19.4% FY24, 19.3% FY23) — the inflection came in 2H 2025 as price + DMC mix landed. The model walks margin to 21.2% FY26e and 21.7% FY27e.

Quarter-to-quarter, operating margin follows a strong seasonal pattern: Q1 trough (mid-teens to high-teens) on negative operating leverage and tariff/inflation absorption, Q2/Q3 peak (22–23%), Q4 normal. Q1 2026 printed 4.3% better adj OP than Q1 2025 on 110 bps of margin expansion — a clean read on the price/cost dynamic before the harder comparisons in 2H.

07 / Bear case

Revenue moved. EPS didn't.

Asymmetry

The Q1 2026 guide raise looks better in the press release than in the math. The sales-growth range moved up +150 bps at both ends (7–9% → 8–11%), data-center growth was re-framed by +800 bps (mid/high-teens → >25%), but the adjusted-EPS range shrank instead of shifting: only the floor moved ($19.15 → $19.30), the ceiling held at $19.85. Midpoint EPS lifted just $0.075 / +0.4% against revenue mid moving +1.5 pts.

The reason is the second bear point: FY26 margin expansion was downgraded. Original Q4 2025 guide was ~+50 bps of adjusted OI margin (Capozzoli, Feb call); Q1 2026 framing is ~+20 bps at the midpoint (Capozzoli, three times during the call). Translated: incremental revenue is coming in at well below the existing margin profile. That is a ~60% downgrade (-30 bps) on the margin walk in a single quarter.

With the stock at $545 (97% of all-time highs) and trading at the upper end of its three-year multiple range, this combination matters. The bull case wants a re-rating on data-center exposure; the bear case is that the market has already priced the revenue raise without seeing it convert through the margin walk.

08 / Valuation

A thin margin of safety.

Multiples

On the model's FY+1 (rolling) EPS of $21.835, HUBB trades at ~24.9× — inside the three-year range (15× – 23×) but at the upper end. Median trailing multiple is ~20×. The stock has roughly doubled from $271 (April 2025) to $545 today on the FY25 EPS over-delivery and the Q4'25 + Q1'26 guide raises.

The model's working target uses 26× on FY27e EPS of $21.835, which prices the stock at $568 vs. $545 spot — ~+4% upside. That is a thin margin of safety; the case requires either margin upside on Aclara recovery / DMC integration or a continued multiple expansion as data-center exposure becomes more visible.
