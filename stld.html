01 / The setup

Two stories, one ticker.

Thesis

STLD operates four segments: Steel Operations (the mini-mill franchise — flat-rolled at Sinton/Butler/Columbus, structural at Roanoke), Metals Recycling (OmniSource — captive scrap supply that backstops the steel cycle), Steel Fabrication (joists and decking — late-cycle construction), and the new Aluminum Operations (rolling mill in Columbus, MS plus slab/recycling in Mexico — first commercial shipments in late 2024).

The Q1 2026 print was the cleanest steel beat of the cycle: record steel shipments, ASP +$86/t to $1,193, adjusted EBITDA +38% Q/Q to $700M, and a 6% dividend raise (the 14th consecutive year). Q4'25's outage trough is fully unwound; the segment is now running at 89% utilization vs. the AISI industry rate of 77%. Tariffs on Mexican slab (50%) have been reframed as a structural support for U.S. lower-carbon production rather than a near-term headwind.

The other story is aluminum. After spending 2025 missing every near-term profitability marker ($-37M Q1 → $-40M expected Q4 → $-47M actual Q4 → $-65M Q1 2026), management's commitment is now stripped down to: shipments and earnings will "increase sharply in Q2." The second tandem cold mill and second CASH line (the gating equipment for the 90% YE exit rate) both slipped from a Q1 commissioning to Q3 — converting the ramp into a back-half hockey stick.

"Combined with Sinton and our four value-add lines, our aluminum operations are expected to add $1.4 billion+ in through-cycle consolidated annual EBITDA." Q1 2026 Earnings Presentation · April 20, 2026 · 5th consecutive event reaffirmed
02 / Revenue

Five segments, two engines.

Composition

Steel Operations is the engine: $13.4B FY25 revenue at a $1.1B operating income (8.1% margin in a trough year — historical peak ~17%). Metals Recycling adds $4.3B of revenue at modest standalone profitability but its strategic value is the captive scrap supply. Aluminum is the new growth engine — only $474M in FY25 revenue, ramping to a model-implied $1.2B FY26e and $1.8B FY27e as production scales toward the ~600kt finished-product run-rate at 90% utilization.

The model carries FY26 net sales at $21.4B (+18%) and FY27 at $22.8B (+7%), with EPS jumping from $6.92 FY25 → $14.00 FY26e → $16.98 FY27e — a doubling of FY25 earnings that requires steel ASP holding ~$1,200, aluminum swinging from a $-65M Q1 26 loss to ~$0 EBITDA by year-end, and Sinton operating at the $475–525M through-cycle EBITDA target.

03 / Steel

The cleanest cycle print in two years.

Drivers

Q1 2026 Steel Operations delivered 3.6Mt of shipments — an all-time record — at an average realized price of $1,193/t, $86/t above Q4 2025. Operating income jumped 73% sequentially to $557M. The Q4'25 outage drag (140–150kt of lost flat-rolled volume) fully unwound, and Sinton ran at record total flat-roll shipments after a year of vendor-disruption recovery.

The structural support is 89% mill utilization vs. an industry rate of 77% (per AISI) — a 12pt gap that reflects STLD's lower-cost EAF position and higher value-add mix. Mexican slab tariffs at 50%, originally framed as a 2025 headwind, have been reframed across the last four quarters as a structural tailwind for domestic lower-carbon production.

04 / Aluminum

A ramp that keeps slipping one quarter.

Risk

Aluminum is the credibility lightning rod. The investment thesis requires aluminum to contribute $650–$700M of through-cycle EBITDA at maturity — about half of the $1.4B+ bundle that anchors the model. The execution path has been a string of slipped near-term commitments:

  • Q1 2025 promise: "positive H2 2025 EBITDA"
  • Q2 2025 walk-back: "monthly EBITDA+ before YE 2025"
  • Q3 2025 walk-back: "Q4 EBITDA breakeven-or-better"
  • Q4 2025 partial-hit: "Achieved December only on 10kt"
  • Q1 2026 walk-back: "Shipments and earnings increase sharply in Q2"

Q1 2026 aluminum operating loss widened to $65M on 22kt of shipments — vs $47M loss on 14kt in Q4 2025. The two pieces of equipment that gate the 90% YE exit-rate (the second tandem cold mill and second CASH line) both slipped from Q1 to Q3 2026 commissioning. The ramp is now mechanically a back-half hockey stick.

05 / Earnings

Doubling FY25 earnings — if the bridge holds.

Bridge

Adjusted EBITDA fell from $3.6B in FY23 (cycle peak) to $2.1B in FY25 (trough) on weaker steel pricing, the Sinton ramp drag, the aluminum startup losses, and Steel Fabrication mean-reversion. Q1 2026's $700M print already runs at a $2.8B annualized EBITDA pace, leaving a clear path to the model's FY26e $3.4B if Q2 delivers on the aluminum step-up.

The longer arc is the $1.4B+ through-cycle EBITDA bundle (Sinton $475–525M + four value-add lines + Aluminum $650–700M). Reaffirmed verbatim across five consecutive events — the load-bearing thesis number. With FY25 LTM EBITDA at $2.4B (per the Q1 26 deck), FY26 needs to start showing the bundle in reported EBITDA: 2026 is the "show me" year.

06 / Margins

A cyclical OI margin recovering off the trough.

Cycle

Consolidated OI margin walked from 16.8% FY23 (cycle peak) → 11.1% FY24 → 8.1% FY25 (trough) as scrap costs held while ASPs fell, and the Sinton + aluminum startup drag absorbed cash. Q1 2026 lifted to 10.3% — back to FY24 levels — and the model walks to 12.8% FY26e and 13.7% FY27e on the spread expansion plus aluminum loss-narrowing.

Two things to flag. First, the FY27e 13.7% is materially below the FY23 peak of 16.8% — the model is not assuming a return to peak pricing. Second, the quarter-to-quarter pattern is now driven less by seasonality and more by aluminum loss cadence: Q1 2026 ran 10.3% with a $-65M aluminum drag; if aluminum hits Q2 swing toward breakeven, Q2 OI margin steps to ~13%.

07 / Valuation

A cyclical at the top of its multiple range.

Multiples

STLD trades at 12.2× EV/FY+1 EBITDA on the model's $3.78B FY27e number — above the three-year median of 11.5× but well below the 16.5× peak (early 2024) and far above the 9.1× trough (Q1 2025). The stock has rallied from $169 (Q1'26 quarter-end) to $229 (current) — +35% in five weeks on the post-earnings rally.

The model's working target uses 13× on FY27e EBITDA of $3,776M, which prices the stock at $265 vs. $229 spot — ~+15.6% upside. The bear case sits at 10× on FY26e EBITDA of $3,365M — implying $159 (−31% downside) if aluminum continues to slip and the cycle turns. For a producer trading near cycle-high multiples on a doubling-EBITDA forecast, the bear-bull skew is wide.
