01 / The setup

A specialty contractor with a multi-decade tailwind, priced for it.

Thesis

Quanta is the largest independent specialty electrical-construction platform in North America — ~22,000 craft linemen, the only non-utility operator with a real helicopter and energized-line fleet, and decade-long MSA relationships with the top ~15 U.S. investor-owned utilities. After the 2021 Blattner (renewable EPC), 2024 Cupertino Electric (data-center electrical) and July 2025 Dynamic Systems (data-center mechanical) acquisitions, the platform now spans the full electrical stack — outside-the-fence transmission, inside-the-fence switchgear, and process-mechanical systems — under one P&L.

The Q1 2026 print delivered +51% YoY adjusted EPS with zero acquisitions — the cleanest read on underlying organic compounding the company has produced. Backlog hit a record $48.5B (+37% YoY) on broad-based MSA demand, with no single project >25% of the $4.5B sequential add. Management raised the FY26 EPS guide $0.90 / +7% at the midpoint to $13.55–$14.25, repeating the FY25 cadence of three sequential raises ending ~$0.70 above the original mid.

The 2030 framework anchors the long-range case: Adj EPS $21.60–$26.75 by 2030 (15–20% CAGR), a "doubling of earnings power" from the FY25 base. The model carries FY26e $14.25 and FY27e $17.40 — at the top end of guide and well above consensus.

"Based on the continued momentum evidenced by our record $48.5 billion of backlog, the strong performance during the quarter and improved visibility into the remainder of the year, we are raising our full year financial expectations." Jayshree Desai, CFO · Q1 2026 earnings call (Apr 30, 2026)
02 / Revenue

Two segments, one engine.

Composition

Quanta reports two GAAP segments. Electric Infrastructure Solutions (Electric & Renewable) is the engine: $23.0B FY25 revenue at a 10.3% adj OI margin, growing 21% YoY and absorbing the entire data-center growth complex. Underground Utility & Infrastructure (Pipeline & Underground) is the smaller, structurally lower-margin sister at $5.5B FY25 revenue at 7.3% adj OI margin — gas LDC replacement, midstream, integrity work.

The model carries FY26 revenue at $35.2B (+24%) at the top of the management $34.7–$35.2B raised range, and FY27 at $38.2B (+8.6%), anchored to the 2030 framework's 7–10% organic CAGR with the FY26 raise front-loading the early years. Note Q1 2026 revenue rose sequentially from Q4 2025 ($7.87B vs $7.84B) — Duke Austin explicitly flagged structural seasonality flattening.

03 / Backlog

The number that is the thesis.

Visibility

Total backlog ended Q1 2026 at $48.5B (+37% YoY, +10% QoQ). The 12-month coverage book sits at $28.2B — implying 80% of FY26 revenue is already booked at the year's start. Book-to-bill ran 1.6× in Q1 2026, the strongest in the trailing five quarters. Across the eight-quarter window, book-to-bill has averaged ~1.3× — the gap is forward revenue compounding ahead of the print.

Two compositional points matter. First, the Q1 build was broad-based, not chunky — no single award >25% of the $4.5B sequential add, MSA-driven across every discipline. Second, the long-promised 765kV Texas booking finally landed — <$1B as an MSA inside the broader add — after slipping three times across 2025. Goalpost stopped moving; credibility partially restored.

04 / Demand

Data center, plus everything else.

Drivers

Three demand vectors compound into the order book. Hyperscaler / large-load (Cupertino + Dynamic Systems platform) — Duke Austin reframed the FY26 growth from +70% YoY at the Q4 2025 introduction to +110% YoY at the Q1 2026 call, citing M&A plus organic. Transmission super-cycle — EEI utility T&D capex stepping from ~$175B in 2024 to >$200B/yr through 2028 (transmission alone $30–35B/yr). And renewable + interconnect — Blattner platform, ~$5–6B FY25 revenue, structurally tied to IRA tax-credit policy.

The flagship project is the NiSource 3 GW JV in Indiana — a multi-discipline, multi-year build with air permits expected mid-2026, backlog conversion late 2026 / 2027, and revenue 2027–28. It is not yet in the $48.5B figure. Pacing check: if air permits don't land by mid-2026, the 2027–28 revenue contribution slips and the 2030 anchor's pace math degrades.

05 / Earnings

Compounding to a doubling by 2030.

Trajectory

Adjusted EPS has compounded from $7.18 in FY23 to $10.75 in FY25 — a 22.4% CAGR. Management's Q1 2026 raised FY26 guide of $13.55–$14.25 implies +29% YoY at the midpoint after one quarter, repeating the FY25 sandbagger pattern. The model carries FY26e at $14.25 (the high end) and FY27e at $17.40.

The 2030 anchor — set at the March 31, 2026 Investor Day — is $21.60–$26.75. From the FY25 $10.75 base, the high end implies a 20.0% CAGR; the mid implies 17.6%; the low 14.4%. A "doubling of earnings power" from FY25 lands at $21.50, reachable in the low scenario. The implicit pace check from FY26 mid $13.90 → 2030 mid $24.18 is 14.8% CAGR over the remaining 4 years — in-frame for the 15–20% target.

06 / Margins

A 6% operating-margin business with 20%+ EPS growth.

Mechanics

PWR is structurally a low-operating-margin business — consolidated FY25 OI margin was 5.7% — but the EPS engine compounds via revenue scale, mix shift toward higher-margin Electric work, and modest operating leverage. The model walks consolidated OI margin from 5.7% FY25 → 6.1% FY26e → 6.6% FY27e. The 2030 framework targets 10.5% adjusted EBITDA margin (vs ~10.2% FY26e implied), implying gradual structural lift, not a step function.

The Underground & Infra "double digits" margin commitment is the most credibility-relevant open item. Original 2025 framing was 8.0–8.5% adj OI; quietly trimmed to 7–8% mid-year; FY25 actual landed at 7.3%. Q1 2026 Duke Austin re-introduced the "double digits" framing, with UI margin lift cited as the principal Q1 earnings driver. A sub-9% UI print in Q2 would re-open the credibility gap.

07 / Valuation

The stock is doing the most of the work.

Multiples

On the model's FY+1 (rolling) EPS of $17.40, PWR trades at 41.8× — the high end of the three-year range (17.8× – 41.8×) and far above the median of 24.2× and average of 25.8×. The stock has gone from $254 a year ago to $728 today as the FY26 raise, the 2030 framework and the data-center re-rating all landed within four months.

The model's working target uses 45× on FY27e EPS of $17.40, which prices the stock at $783 vs. $728 spot — ~+7.6% upside. That is a thin margin of safety; the case requires either a continued raise pattern through Q2/Q3 (FY26 prints $14.50+ instead of $14.25) or an extension of the 45× multiple as the 2030 framework gets rebased upward at the next Investor Day.
