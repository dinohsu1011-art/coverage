01 / The setup

A power-management compounder with one very large tailwind.

Thesis

Eaton is an Irish-domiciled, U.S.-managed industrial that has spent two decades rotating from truck axles toward electrical equipment. After the 2012 Cooper acquisition and the 2017/2021 divestitures of lighting and hydraulics, Electrical and Aerospace now generate ~88% of revenue and ~93% of segment profit. The remaining Vehicle and eMobility businesses will be spun off into a separately-listed Mobility company by end of Q1 2027.

The 2026 plan is a clean three-line guide: organic revenue +7–9%, segment margin 24.6–25.0%, adjusted EPS $13.00–$13.50. What makes this guide unusual is the gap between the headline numbers and the order book underneath: Electrical Americas Q4 orders rose more than 50% YoY and the segment ended 2025 with a record $13.2B backlog (+31% YoY). Inside that, data-center orders grew ~200% YoY in Q4.

Two known frictions sit on top of that order strength: a 130 bps margin drag in 2026 from $1.5B of capacity expansion (24 sites, front-loaded into Q1), and a paused buyback to fund the pending Boyd Thermal close — which lifts data-center content from ~$2.9M to ~$3.4M per MW.

"U.S. large data center construction backlog stands at 206 GW — eleven years at 2025 build rates." Management, Q4 2025 Earnings Call
02 / Revenue

Five segments, two engines.

Composition

Electrical Americas is the engine: $13.3B of FY25 revenue at a 29.9% segment margin, growing 16.1% in 2025 with data center as the disproportionate driver inside it. Aerospace quietly compounds at low-teens organic with a 23.8% margin and a record $4.3B backlog. Electrical Global is the smaller international twin, finally inflecting in 2025 (+9.1% on European stabilization). Mobility is in managed decline ahead of the spin.

The model carries FY26 revenue at $29.9B (+8.9%) and FY27 at $32.5B (+8.9%), both anchored to the management 2026 guide and the long-range 2030 framework (6–9% organic, 28% segment margin, 12%+ EPS CAGR).

03 / Data center

The single largest swing factor in the model.

Demand

Data center is roughly 21% of FY25 sales and grew ~49% in the Americas. The quarterly print accelerated through 2025: orders +70% YoY in Q3, then +200% YoY in Q4. Book-to-bill in the Electrical Americas data-center vertical ran 1.7x in Q3 — backlog is building, not converting.

Eaton's content per MW is rising from ~$2.9M today to ~$3.4M after Boyd Thermal closes, capturing the inner-loop liquid cooling layer (cold plates, CDUs) that sits closest to the chips. The Investor Day TAM range was $1.2–2.9M/MW; current portfolio is already at the high end, and the post-Boyd trajectory pushes through it.

The mix is also shifting. FY25 orders were ~50/50 AI versus cloud, but FY25 revenue recognition was still ~30% AI / 70% cloud — i.e. the AI-driven backlog is still in front of revenue, not behind it.

04 / Backlog

Multi-year visibility, not a single quarter's miracle.

Coverage

Combined Electrical Americas + Aerospace backlog ended 2025 at $19.6B, up from $3.8B at year-end 2019. Electrical Americas alone holds $13.2B (+31% YoY) with book-to-bill of ~1.2x. Aerospace runs a $4.3B backlog (+16% YoY).

Beyond the backlog, Eaton tracks an internal database of capital projects ≥$1B across North America. The pipeline is now $3.0T spanning 866 projects, with data centers 54% of YTD announcements and the rest mostly U.S. reshoring. Projects start at a ~16% rate; Eaton wins ~40% of what it bids on; megaproject revenue grew ~30% in 2025 and converts over 3–5 years.

05 / Earnings

EPS compounds at low double-digits through the spin.

Drivers

Adjusted EPS has compounded at ~15% from $9.12 in FY23 to $12.07 in FY25. Management's 2026 guide of $13.00–$13.50 is +10% at the midpoint despite the capacity-ramp margin drag and a paused buyback (~$0 of repurchases to fund Boyd). The model carries FY26e at $13.50 and FY27e at $15.30, in line with the long-range 12%+ EPS CAGR framework.

A point worth flagging: 2026 EPS growth is essentially all operational — no buyback help, no FX, no Boyd revenue (deal hasn't closed). If revenue ramps faster than the cost base in H2, the high end of guide is achievable; if the H1 ramp slips, the trough extends.

06 / Valuation

Trading at the top of its history.

Multiples

On the model's FY+1 (rolling) EPS, ETN trades at 28.3x — at the top of the three-year range (15.9x – 28.3x), versus a median of 25.9x and an average of 23.8x. The re-rating happened in 2024 as the data-center order book broke higher; the question is whether that multiple holds while EPS is doing the work in 2026.

The model's working target uses 30x on FY27e EPS of $15.30, which prices the stock at $459 vs. $433 spot — ~6% upside. That is a thin margin of safety; the case requires either margin upside on Boyd integration or a higher long-range multiple tied to a cleaner post-spin mix.
